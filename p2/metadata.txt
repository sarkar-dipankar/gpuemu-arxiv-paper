Abstract:
Mixed-precision GPU kernels are validated against a per-(op, dtype) absolute tolerance (atol) typically hand-picked to two-significant-figure precision. We show empirically that this hand-picked atol is systematically 100x to 1,700x looser than the kernel's actual error envelope, which causes the standard correctness oracle (allclose) to silently accept genuinely buggy kernels. We propose a one-shot calibration: take the 95th percentile of max-abs-error across correct controls per (op, dtype), multiply by 1.5 as a safety margin, and use that as the calibrated atol. On the gpuemu 24-op corpus, calibrated atol raises bug-detection recall from 65 percent to 82 percent (+23 pp absolute), at the cost of 8 borderline false positives out of 1,016 correct cases (0.7 pp). The calibration runs in seconds against accumulated B2 run records; the headline tightening factors (100x-1,747x) suggest hand-picked tolerances across the LLM-kernel ecosystem are routinely far looser than the operator's intrinsic error envelope.
